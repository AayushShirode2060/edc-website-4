    INTRODUCTION


    Since decades Indian students have been enamoured by the international universities. New experiences, new vistas, new horizons - attract the young Indian boys and girls, year after year.

    THE CHALLENGES
    Despite seismic shifts in diplomatic relations between nations and stricter immigration laws, the allure of international education remains strong for Indian students. Even after enduring the chaos brought on by the COVID-19 bans, the conflict between Russia and Ukraine, and the diplomatic deadlock with Canada, earning a degree from a foreign university is worthwhile.

    The number dynamics
    Based on University Living's survey Beyond Beds and Boundaries: Indian Student Mobility Report 2023, the number of Indian students studying overseas is predicted to reach two million by 2025, up from about a million in 2019. Additionally, it was discovered that Indian students directly spent $47 billion on their education abroad in 2022, and that amount is predicted to increase to as much as $70 billion by 2025.

    Changing rules
    India's market for international education primarily targets the United States, Canada, Australia, New Zealand and the United Kingdom. These nations modified their regulations this year in an effort to expedite the application procedure. The United States imposed rigorous checks and penalties on dishonest universities. Depending on the application location, there is a waiting period of 100-325 days for student visas in India. The United Kingdom will only grant dependent visas to foreign students pursuing postgraduate research degrees starting in January 2024, which caused a last-minute rush among this year's visa seekers. Numerous students have decided to postpone their applications from January to September as a result of Canada's diplomatic deadlock with India.

    Popular subjects



    Indian students consistently enroll in postgraduate management courses and undergraduate STEM (science, technology, engineering, and math) degrees.
    International degrees not only help students expand their knowledge at their own pace and get out of their comfort zones, but they also give them an advantage in the job market in India. A growing number of countries are collaborating with foreign institutions to address the labor shortages in their markets. Although the programs are still being developed, the majority of the growth at the undergraduate level appears to be concentrated on AI, blockchain, cyber security, and data science for the upcoming year.

    Role of a trusted consultant
    Indian students living in Tier-2 and Tier-3 cities can now consult with consultants who provide advice on developing a unique personal brand for international university applications. A growing number of Indian students are applying overseas as a result of consultants' assistance with financial aid and scholarship applications. Improving transparency

    Consultancies are using AI and digitalization to counteract the long-standing perception that student visas serve as a backdoor for illegal immigration. Their quality control teams examine the financial stability of applicants. Via the channels, the applicant can also track the status of their documents. Transparency is key to the sector's success.
